The four alerts everyone is waiting for.

The MVP focuses on the high-value moments that reduce confusion immediately. These triggers are fast, urgent, and easy for admins to issue from a single command center.

01 · CHECK-IN OPEN

Report to staging

Alerts athletes that check-in has opened so they can move toward the correct area without delay.

02 · ON DECK

It’s almost time

Notifies parents and athletes that an event is imminent so they can get to the viewing or ready area.

03 · 🔴 LAST CALL

High-urgency final alert

Designed for maximum visibility with urgent messaging and visual cues when check-in is about to close.

04 · FINAL RESULTS

Scores posted instantly

Pushes final times, placements, or outcomes the moment results are available in the system.

How the MeetCheck Operating System flows

The full OS is intentionally simple from the user’s point of view, even though the backend is handling high concurrency, event normalization, and segmented delivery at speed.

1

Meet Created

Admin publishes meet structure, events, heats, brackets, and athlete relationships.

2

Users Follow

Parents and athletes subscribe to events through a streamlined app experience.

3

Status Changes

Admin selects a trigger like Check-In Open, On Deck, Last Call, or Final Results.

4

Engine Broadcasts

Cloud logic matches the event to follower records and sends targeted push alerts.

5

Clarity Delivered

Families get the update immediately and can act before the moment is missed.

Architecture designed for resilience, speed, and scale.

MeetCheck’s backend is engineered around fast writes, targeted fan-out, and a flexible sports data model that can support multiple meet formats without rebuilding the platform each time.

Notification Engine

Firebase Cloud Messaging + Cloud Functions

Admin actions trigger server-side logic that queries the followedEvents array in Firestore and instantly broadcasts the correct payload only to the relevant subscribers.

Data Model

Normalized for universal sports support

Meets → Events → Athletes creates a flexible foundation capable of handling heat-based, rotation-based, and bracket-based competition structures under one platform architecture.

Experience Layer

Offline-first and native-feeling UI

Cached schedules keep the interface useful even in connectivity-poor venues, while the Aura design system keeps interactions clear, fluid, and visually prioritized.

Monetization

Stripe inside the critical path

One-click access models let parents purchase meet-specific visibility or unlock seasonal subscriptions without leaving the product flow or increasing friction.

Roadmap from doctrine to deployment.

MeetCheck starts with ruthless focus on the core communication problem, then expands into a broader intelligence layer for live-event operations.

Phase 1

MVP Notification Core

Admin portal, followed events, the four core push triggers, offline-first schedule access, and meet-specific monetization with Stripe.

Phase 2

Operational Expansion

Broader sport normalization, deeper event-state visibility, improved admin workflow tooling, and higher concurrency optimization at scale.

Phase 3

Digital Twin Intelligence

Native experience, data ingestion from timing systems, and machine-learning powered delay prediction that shifts MeetCheck from reactive utility to predictive infrastructure.
